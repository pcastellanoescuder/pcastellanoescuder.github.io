Biography

I obtained my Bachelor degree in Biology at the University of Barcelona in June 2016. In July 2017 I obtained my Master degree in Bioinformatics and Biostatistics at the University of Barcelona and the Open University of Catalonia (UOC). My master thesis was titled “Prediction model of response to a lifestyle treatment in childhood obesity”.

During my bachelor and master degree studies, I focused my research in the search of epigenetic and metabolomic biomarkers in childhood obesity.

I obtained my PhD at the “Biomarkers and Nutritional & Food Metabolomics” group and “Statistics and Bioinformatics” group at the University of Barcelona. During my PhD, my research was focused on metabolomics, food metabolomics, predictive modeling, machine learning and nutrimetabolomic ontologies.

Currently, I’m a postdoctoral researcher at the Hirschey Lab at the Duke University. My research focuses in omics data integration to address questions about the relationships between different omic fields and data sources, improving knowledge of the relationships between different omics in the complex biological networks of organisms and phenotypes.
